KB-40-A

A low-frills, 40% keyboard kit with a unique stainless steel sheet metal construction. And light-up buttons.

features

Repairable

Composed entirely of readily-available, off-the-shelf components, save the case and PCB. If a part disappears, we can either provide replacements or tell you where to find them.

Sheet metal construction

The KB-40-A uses thin stainless steel to create a solid enclosure with less material. The conventional sheet metal shaping gives the board soft corners and crisp edges.

Simple but substantial

Weighing 0 kg / 0 lbs, the KB-40-A has the heft of a solid aluminum board, but the delicate lines of a folded sheet material.

Light-up buttons

Two illuminated buttons toggle functions like caps lock, audio mute, and layers. We like having one set to "game on!" mode, which prevents your cat from typing when you're away. The buttons latch down with a satisfying, tactile click.

Straightforward assembly

Exposed, accessible screws secure the PCB and case. Lifting the hood to access the switches only requires the removal of four screws located on the front and back.

Sounds like a keyboard

The heavy, stainless steel case gives the hollow case a substantial sound you can shape with the included wool felt and IXPE foam. Here's how it sounds.

Hotswap PCB

An original PCB design by A·Okay! The one piece circuit board features Kailh hotswap sockets, USB-C connectivity, pre-soldered buttons and rotary encoder, and an immersion gold finish. No flex cuts, daughter boards, or tiny cables.

This is our first consumer electronics manufacturing project. We like to do things right and do right by the people that put their trust in us. Should there be enough community interest in this board, we intend to put a small initial offering of units up for sale. Each will be individually numbered and assembled by us by hand.
