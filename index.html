KB-40-A

A low-frills, 40% keyboard kit with a unique stainless steel sheet metal construction. And light-up buttons.

Sheet metal construction

The KB-40-A uses thin stainless steel to create a solid enclosure with less material. The conventional sheet metal shaping gives the board soft corners and crisp edges.

Simple but substantial

Weighing 0 kg / 0 lbs, the KB-40-A has the heft of a solid aluminum board, but the delicate lines of a folded sheet material.

Light-up buttons

Two programmable, illuminated buttons toggle functions like caps lock, audio mute, and microphone mute. We like having one set to "game on!" mode, which lets you know it's go time with a happy green light and prevents your cat from typing when you're away. The buttons latch down with a satisfying tactile click.

Straightforward assembly
Easy access to switches

Eight screws securing the PCB and four securing the case top. The screws are all easily accessible and large enough to be held by human hands. Lifting the hood to access the switches and PCB only requires the removal of four screws, located on the front and back.

But does it thock?

The heavy stainless steel case gives the hollow case a substantial sound. Adding the included wool felt increases clackiness while the IXPE foam gives the board a deeper, marbly sound.

Hotswap PCB

An original PCB design by A·Okay! The one piece circuit board features Kailh hotswap sockets, USB-C connectivity, pre-soldered buttons and rotary encoder, and an immersion gold finish. No flex cuts, daughter boards, or tiny cables.
