KB-40-A

A low-frills, 40% keyboard kit with a unique stainless steel sheet metal construction. And light-up buttons.

Sheet metal construction

The KB-40-A uses thin stainless steel to create a solid enclosure with less material. The conventional sheet metal shaping gives the board soft corners and crisp edges.

Simple but substantial

Weighing in at 0 kg / 0 lbs, the KB-40-A has the heft of a board with a more solid construction. The stainless steel of the case gives the board thin lines while maintaining a substantial weight.

Light-up buttons

Two programmable buttons provide illuminated togglability for when all other lights go out. Functions include caps lock, audio mute, and microphone mute. We like having one set to "game on!" mode, which lets you know it's go time with a happy green light and prevents your cat from typing when you're away. The buttons latch down with a satisfying tactile click.

Straightforward assembly
Easy access to switches

The board assembles like an Erector Set, with eight screws securing the PCB and four securing the case top. The screws are all easily accessible and large enough to be held by human hands. Lifting the hood to access the switches and PCB only requires the removal of four screws on the front and back.

But does it thock?

The heavy stainless steel case gives the board a substantial sound, despite the hollow case. The included wool felt increases clackiness while the IXPE foam gives the board a deep, marbly sound.
